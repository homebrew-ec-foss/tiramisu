> This manifesto serves as a retrospective on the technical achievements and changes brought forth by the '26 batch of HSP during the academic year 2025-26.

# rollback;

Usually manifestos are forward-looking documents that outline the vision and goals for the future. However, this manifesto takes a different approach by reflecting on the past year and celebrating the technical milestones and transformative changes that the '26 batch of HSP brought during 2025-26.

## [Tilde 4.0](/announcements/tilde-4.0)

> Tilde is usually the first big task that sets the ball rolling for the new batch of recruits (including us @Core) and this year was no different. 

We revamped the entire Tilde mentorship program, introducing a more structured recruitment policy and spanning it over 5 weeks during our summer break. We launched the Homebrew Discord server and helped genuinely pick interested mentees who were committed to learning and contributing to open-source projects.

# Odd Semester

Bootstrap week was very fun, we met some really interested folks and loved talking to people in general. We also had some really good workshops and a productive odd semester.

## [Modal Editors](/announcements/modal-editors)

Kiran's workshop on modal editors was a huge hit, with over 100 attendees eager to learn about the power of modal editing. The session covered the core principles of modal editors, demonstrating how they can enhance coding speed and precision. Attendees left with a solid understanding of how to use modal editors in real-world projects, marking a significant step forward in their coding journey.

![Modal Editors Workshop](/static/images/gallery/modal-editors/modal-editors.png)

## Pruning + New Recruitment Policy

Sometimes you have to clean up your garden to let the new plants grow, we had a lot of dead weight in our community, and we wanted to create a more active and engaged membership.

We bid farewell to some of our members that no longer aligned with our vision, while welcoming new recruits who bring fresh perspectives and energy to our community. We got rid of domains and instead focused on recruiting members who are genuinely interested in learning and contributing to open-source projects for we are a tech community after all.

We want to ensure that our members are genuinely interested in learning and contributing to open-source projects, and that they have the necessary skills and mindset to learn and build in public.

# Even Semester

The even semester was a bit more relaxed, with fewer workshops and events on campus. However, we went full steam with firesides, especially with the new recruits and opening it up to the entire community. We had some really good firesides with alum/professionals and our own members, covering a wide range of topics from Networks, Finance, UX, FOSS, Machine Learning, Math and more.

## [Bitfl!p](/announcements/bitflip) Workshop

![BitFlip Workshop Poster](/static/images/gallery/bitflip/poster.jpg)

Andey and Aston bring you *BitFlip,* an interactive workshop on the *Theory of Encoding Data*, where we dive into the world of data compression and error coding, exploring the algorithms that power data storage, transfer, and modern communication systems.

## Branding

Its time for new Hoodies! A homebrew theme'd hoodie and a niche design that reflects our community and this is something that can be worn casually in an office or public space without it reeking of a college club. 

We want to create a sense of pride and belonging among our members, and we also want to showcase our community to the world. Our unique beige hoodies with a simple design and our logo will be a great way to achieve this, and we hope to see our members rocking them in the wild!

### FOSS Talks 3.0

> FOSS Talks is back with its 3rd edition, and this time we hosted it at the iconic Samagata Space in Bengaluru!

## Meetups

Meetups are a great way to connect with other developers and learn about new technologies and trends in the industry. We encourage our members to attend meetups and conferences, both locally and globally, to expand their knowledge and network. Some of the talks our members attended include:

### IndiaFOSS '25

We had a great time at IndiaFOSS '25, where we got to spectate some amazing talks and meet some really cool people in the FOSS ecosystem, both India and around the world. 

## Looking Ahead

This year rebooting or rather expanding Homebrew to beyond just being a collection of open-source projects to a more holistic FOSS wing was a big change. We don't know if it will work but we want to host meetups and events under the Homebrew banner, and we want to encourage our members to talk to people in the industry and bring in more speakers to our events.

![FOSS Talks 3.0 audience and venue](/static/images/gallery/fosstalks-3/fosstalks.JPG)

> HSP is a student run community, and as such, we are always evolving and changing. We are not afraid to make changes that we believe will benefit our community, even if they are difficult or unpopular. 

We hope the next core team continues to build on the foundation we have laid and takes HSP to new heights. We are excited to see what the future holds for HSP and are confident that it will continue to be a vibrant and thriving community for years to come!
