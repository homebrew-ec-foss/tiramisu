> This manifesto serves as a retrospective on the technical achievements and changes brought forth by the '26 batch of HSP during the academic year 2025-26.

# rollback;

Usually manifestos are forward-looking documents that outline the vision and goals for the future. However, this manifesto takes a different approach by reflecting on the past year and celebrating the technical milestones and transformative changes that the '26 batch of HSP brought during 2025-26.

## [Tilde 4.0](/announcements/tilde-4.0)

> Tilde is usually the first big task that sets the ball rolling for the new batch of recruits (including us @Core) and this year was no different. 

We revamped the entire Tilde mentorship program, introducing a more structured recruitment policy and spanning it over 5 weeks during our summer break. We launched the Homebrew Discord server and helped genuinely pick interested mentees who were committed to learning and contributing to open-source projects.

## Pruning + New Recruitment Policy

Sometimes growth requires pruning. We bid farewell to some of our members that no longer aligned with our vision, while welcoming new recruits who bring fresh perspectives and energy to our community. This also was a result of a lot of dead weight in the community, and we hope to maintain a more active and engaged membership moving forward. 

We also introduced a new recruitment policy that emphasizes tech, for we are a technical community after all. We want to ensure that our members are genuinely interested in learning and contributing to open-source projects, and that they have the necessary skills and mindset to thrive in our community.

## Odd Semester

Bootstrap week was very fun, we met some really interested folks and loved talking to people in general. We also had some really good workshops during the odd semester

### [Project Expo 2.0](/announcements/project-expo-2)

> We had 419 registrations and 250+ attendees filling Seminar Hall 3 and a bunch of classrooms; showcasing some amazing projects:

    - Mettu: A static site generator using Python and Vite. By Andey Hemanth
    - FeynTerm: A learning pipeline using the Feynman Learning Technique. By Avrit Sharma
    - Plus Boy: An ARM7TDMI emulator written in C++. By Aditya R
    - Home-Labbing 101: A guide to self-hosting websites, NAS, and Home Assistant. By Mebin Thattil
    - HALO VR: A modular VR system with smart shoes, a haptic suit, and motion tracking. By Zaid Mallik
    - TestRunner: A sandboxed coding environment for secure, one-time test runs. By Vinaayak G Dasika

## [Modal Editors](/announcements/modal-editors)

Kiran's workshop on modal editors was a huge hit, with over 100 attendees eager to learn about the power of modal editing. The session covered the core principles of modal editors, demonstrating how they can enhance coding speed and precision. Attendees left with a solid understanding of how to use modal editors in real-world projects, marking a significant step forward in their coding journey.

## Even Semester

The even semester was a bit more relaxed, with fewer workshops and events on campus. However, we went full steam with firesides, especially with the new recruits and opening it up to the entire community. We had some really good firesides with alum/professionals and our own members, covering a wide range of topics from Networks, Finance, UX, FOSS, Machine Learning, Math and more.

### Firesides

We encourage every one of our members to share their knowledge and experiences with the community, and we are always looking for interesting topics and speakers for our 
firesides.

## What Changed

HSP is a student run community, and as such, we are always evolving and changing. We are not afraid to make changes that we believe will benefit our community, even if they are difficult or unpopular. We are committed to creating a community that is inclusive, supportive, and focused on learning and growth.

This year rebooting or rather expanding Homebrew to beyond just being a collection of open-source projects to a more holistic FOSS wing was a big change. We don't know if it will work but we want to host meetups and events under the Homebrew banner, and we want to encourage our members to talk to people in the industry and bring in more speakers to our events.

We hope the next core team continues to build on the foundation we have laid and takes HSP to new heights. We are excited to see what the future holds for HSP and are confident that it will continue to be a vibrant and thriving community for years to come.
